Cleanup task on /amex: replace the "Ending balance · Trailing 12 months" chart with a forward-looking version pinned to a fixed window that rolls forward by month.

CONTEXT — same pattern as the /chase chart already shipped (task #785, "actual vs forecast"). That work extended BalanceTrendChart at artifacts/h2budget/src/components/account-page/balance-trend-chart.tsx to support multi-series, date-keyed rendering with a today marker. We reuse that infrastructure here — single series only.

DESIGN:

WINDOW
- Start = max(2026-05-01, current month start). The current month is the left edge.
- End = start + 12 months − 1 day (so the chart shows exactly 12 months).
- Rolling: on the first of each new month, the window slides forward one month. So in Jun 2026 the window becomes Jun 2026 → May 2027, in Jul 2026 it becomes Jul 2026 → Jun 2027, etc.

DATA
- Single series: actual Amex ending balance per week (Sun-Sat buckets), bounded by the window above.
- Today is visually marked on the chart (vertical ReferenceLine or shaded background change — same as the /chase version).
- No forecast line. Credit card spending isn't reasonably forecastable, so we just plot real history as it accumulates.
- TRIM TO REAL DATA. If the last Amex sync delivered points up to today (e.g. May 29 2026), the line ends at the today marker. Do NOT carry the last value forward as a flat line into future months. The right portion of the chart is genuinely blank until future syncs populate it.

CHART TITLE / SUBTITLE
- Caption: "ENDING BALANCE — forward 12 months"
- Right-aligned subtitle: window range, e.g. "May 2026 – Apr 2027". Use the same date-formatting helper the trailing chart uses today.

OTHER BEHAVIORS — don't break these
- The per-card chip filter at the top (All / Blue Cash / Delta Gold / Platinum) still scopes the chart to the selected card.
- The per-card Amex ending balance numbers above the chart stay unchanged.
